# Steve K

Retired technology worker, systems thinker, and independent AI researcher exploring conscience-guided artificial intelligence.

I am currently developing **Haven**, a private experimental AI architecture focused on:

* conscience-guided response shaping
* truth and transparency boundaries
* temporal orientation
* emotional context awareness
* continuity and memory design
* human-centered safety
* dignity-preserving AI interaction

Haven is built around a simple belief:

> Intelligence without conscience is only capability.
> Intelligence with conscience may become something worth trusting.

## About Haven

Haven is a private research project exploring how artificial intelligence systems might be guided by principles of care, restraint, honesty, continuity, and moral reflection.

The work includes private experiments involving gateway design, model orchestration, emotional telemetry, symbolic state mapping, and long-term memory architecture.

The core repositories are private while the system is under active development. Public materials may be released later as sanitized documentation, design notes, essays, or safety-oriented summaries.

## Current Focus

I am especially interested in:

* AI alignment beyond rule enforcement
* conscience as a runtime guidance layer
* emotionally aware but evidence-bounded systems
* memory systems that preserve context without violating privacy
* AI companionship grounded in honesty and care
* building technology that helps people care for one another

## Why This Matters

AI should not only be powerful.

It should be careful.

It should be honest about what it knows and what it does not know.

It should respect human dignity, privacy, and emotional safety.

Haven is my attempt to explore what that kind of system could become.
